## What Needs Improvement

### 1. Color Palette — Medical Trust Problem (Critical)
**Score impact: -1.5 points**

Pink-to-purple is aesthetically pleasant but creates a **trust mismatch for a medical platform**. Medical and health-tech industry leaders deliberately use:
- Blue (trust, clinical precision) — WebMD, Medscape
- White/off-white with green accents (cleanliness, growth) — Khan Academy, Brilliant
- Deep navy + vibrant accent (authority + energy) — Coursera, edX

Pink reads as playful and feminine-leaning, which may alienate a significant portion of medical students. Duolingo uses this palette intentionally because its brand IS playful learning. A medical education platform carries implicit expectations of seriousness and credibility.

**Recommendation:** Shift to a deep blue or navy base with a vibrant coral or teal accent to retain personality while building trust.

---

### 2. Floating Decorative Circles — Visual Noise (Significant)
**Score impact: -1.2 points**

The large floating circles scattered across the background and foreground (bottom-left, bottom-center, top-center, etc.) serve no communicative purpose. They:
- Fragment visual focus
- Compete with the main illustration
- Feel like placeholder design elements that were never removed

Compare to industry leaders: Notion uses **zero** decorative noise in their hero — every pixel earns its place. Framer uses abstract shapes only when they reinforce the product concept (motion, fluidity).

**Recommendation:** Remove or reduce to one purposeful background shape. If blobs are needed for depth, push them to the far background with very low opacity (5–10%) and blur them.

---

### 3. Illustration Quality — Generic and Disconnected
**Score impact: -0.8 points**

The cell biology illustration in the blue circle is conceptually relevant (medicine = biology) but execution is flat and clip-art-like. It also floats without visual grounding — there's no shadow, depth, or integration with the surrounding layout.

Industry leaders at this level either:
- Use custom 3D renders (Coursera, Google)
- Use clean, brand-consistent iconography/illustrations (Duolingo's Duo character, Khan Academy's mascot)
- Use real product screenshots (Linear, Notion, Vercel)

A product screenshot or an animated preview of the actual test-taking interface would be infinitely more persuasive than a generic cell diagram.

**Recommendation:** Replace with an animated or static product mockup showing a real test question or procedural learning screen. Show the product, sell the product.

---

### 4. Subheading Text — Too Small, Too Weak
**Score impact: -0.6 points**

"Rozpocznij swoją podróż w świecie medycyny z naszą innowacyjną platformą edukacyjną."

This text is rendered at approximately 14–15px and has low contrast against the pink background. Problems:
- "Podróż" (journey) is vague marketing language — says nothing specific
- The font size makes it skippable
- Low contrast fails accessibility guidelines (WCAG AA requires 4.5:1 for normal text)

Compare to Coursera's subheading: *"Join 148 million learners. Explore over 7,000 courses from world-class universities."* — specific, credibility-loaded, scannable.

**Recommendation:** Increase font size to 18–20px, improve contrast, and rewrite to include a specific benefit or differentiator. Example: *"Interaktywne testy, algorytmy kliniczne i fiszki — wszystko w jednym miejscu."*

---

### 5. Headline Value Proposition — Vague
**Score impact: -0.5 points**

"Edukacja medyczna może być jeszcze łatwiejsza." — *Medical education can be even easier.*

This is a benefit statement, not a unique value proposition. Every competitor can claim the same thing. The headline doesn't answer: **easier than what? Easier how? For whom specifically?**

Compare to Linear's hero: *"The issue tracker built for modern software teams."* — role-specific, product-specific, benefit-specific in one sentence.

**Recommendation:** Anchor the headline to a specific pain point or audience. Example:
*"Zdaj LEK i LDEK — z platformą zaprojektowaną przez studentów medycyny."*

---

### 6. Social Proof Depth — Surface Level
**Score impact: -0.3 points**

6,500+ students is a solid number but it stands alone. Industry leaders layer multiple proof points:
- Star ratings ("4.8 ★ from 12,000 reviews")
- Institutional logos ("Used by students at WUM, GUMed, CM UJ")
- Outcome stats ("87% passed LEK on first attempt")

**Recommendation:** Add at least one outcome-based stat or a university logo strip below the fold.
