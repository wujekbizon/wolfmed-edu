## What Works Well

### 1. CTA Hierarchy — Strong
The two-button system ("Rozpocznij naukę" + "Zobacz jak to działa →") is textbook correct. Filled primary + ghost secondary creates a clear decision path without pressure. Comparable to Duolingo and Coursera's approach.

### 2. Animated Living Cell — Brand Differentiator (clarified)
A breathing, animated cell is genuinely distinctive. No major edtech competitor uses a living biological organism as their hero visual. When done well, animation communicates "alive, dynamic, modern" — which maps directly onto the brand promise. The concept is strong. The static screenshot does it a disservice.

### 3. Headline Keyword Highlighting
Coral accent on "medyczna" pulls the eye and anchors the domain immediately. Works well against the dark type.

### 4. Social Proof Placement
Avatar stack + student count sits directly below CTAs — right where conversion friction needs to be reduced. The placement is correct.

### 5. Navigation Design
Centered nav with active pill indicator, logo left, avatar right — clean, modern, no friction.

### 6. "Łatwiejsza" — Tool-Backed Promise (clarified)
Now that "easier" is understood as a direct reference to the platform's tools (tests, algorithms, flashcards, etc.), the headline reads as a verifiable claim rather than a hollow tagline. It invites the question "how?" — which the page below presumably answers. That's good narrative architecture.

### 7. Scroll Indicator
Mouse icon at bottom center — subtle, correct, professional.

## What Still Needs Fixing

### 1. Typography — Most Urgent Remaining Issue
**Priority: High**

The subheading "Rozpocznij swoją podróż w świecie medycyny z naszą innowacyjną platformą edukacyjną." has two compounding problems:

**Size:** Approximately 14–15px — too small to anchor after a 60px+ headline. There is a jarring size gap between headline and subtext with no intermediate weight to bridge them.

**Contrast:** Light gray/dark text on a saturated pink background produces poor contrast. WCAG AA requires 4.5:1 ratio for normal text — this likely fails.

**Content:** "Podróż" (journey) is still the weakest word on the page. Now that we know the platform has specific tools, name them here.

**Recommended rewrite:**
> *"Interaktywne testy, algorytmy kliniczne i notatki — wszystko czego potrzebujesz do zdania LEK."*

This turns a vague journey metaphor into a concrete tool inventory — supporting the "easier via tools" headline promise.

**Fix:** Increase to 17–19px, darken text or lighten background behind it, rewrite to name specific tools.

---

### 2. Gradient Saturation — Dialed Too High
**Priority: Medium**

The original intent was a **very light pinkish gradient** — delicate, warm, approachable. What shipped reads as bold magenta-to-purple, which carries more "beauty app" energy than "medical education" energy.

The concept itself is defensible. A soft warm gradient as background:
- Differentiates from the clinical blue-white standard
- Feels welcoming rather than sterile
- Works with the bio dome visual language

The execution went too far. Pull the saturation back significantly — the gradient should whisper, not shout. Think: barely-there blush, not lipstick pink.

**Fix:** Reduce gradient saturation by ~40–60%, keep the hue direction but make it near-white with just a trace of warmth.

---

### 3. Bio Dome Floating Shapes — Concept Right, Execution Loud
**Priority: Medium**

The ecosystem framing is a genuinely interesting design concept. A living cell surrounded by a bio dome of floating particles makes thematic sense for a biology-anchored medical platform.

The problem is scale and opacity. Currently the shapes compete for attention at the same visual weight as the illustration itself. They should be atmospheric — background texture — not foreground elements.

Reference: Apple's particle backgrounds, Stripe's gradient orbs — both use this technique but at 5–15% opacity so they never pull focus from the primary content.

**Fix options:**
- Reduce opacity to 15–25% and apply a slight blur
- Scale down the circle sizes by 30–40%
- Ensure all decorative shapes sit visually *behind* the text and illustration z-layers
- Consider giving the shapes a very slow, gentle drift animation — reinforces the "living ecosystem" concept without being distracting

---

### 4. Social Proof — Needs a Second Layer
**Priority: Low**

6,500+ students is a good number. Add one more layer:
- An outcome stat: *"87% zdało za pierwszym razem"*
- Or university logos: WUM, GUMed, CM UJ
- Or a single pull-quote from a student

---

## Revised Priority Fix List

1. **[High — Typography]** Increase subheading size, improve contrast, rewrite copy to name specific tools
2. **[Medium — Gradient]** Pull saturation back to original light pinkish intent (~40–60% reduction)
3. **[Medium — Shapes]** Reduce opacity and scale of bio dome particles; optionally add slow drift animation
4. **[Low — Social proof]** Add outcome stat or university logos

## Final Verdict

With the clarifications understood, this hero section is meaningfully stronger than the first review suggested. The animated living cell is a genuine differentiator — no competitor does this. The "easier via tools" headline is honest and leads the user into the page naturally. The bio dome concept is conceptually rich and worth keeping.

The remaining work is refinement, not reinvention:

- **Typography is the most urgent fix** — the subheading is too small and too vague to carry its weight
- **The gradient needs to return to its original subtlety** — the design intent was right, execution drifted
- **The bio dome shapes need to recede** — atmosphere, not architecture

Fix those three things and this hero sits confidently in the **top 25% of edtech hero sections**. The brand identity is distinctive without being reckless. That's harder to achieve than it looks.

> "The concept is there. Now reduce the volume on everything except the cell and the headline."
